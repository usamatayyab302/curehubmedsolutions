## Why Denial Management Needs Structure

Denials become expensive when teams treat every claim as an isolated problem. A structured workflow helps staff separate high-value appeals from routine corrections.

## Segment Denials by Root Cause

Start by grouping denials into eligibility, authorization, coding, timely filing, and documentation categories. This quickly shows where upstream fixes are needed.

### Focus on Preventable Denials

Preventable denials reveal process failures. If one payer repeatedly rejects claims for missing authorization, registration and clinical teams need a tighter handoff.

## Prioritize by Financial Impact

Not every denial deserves the same urgency. Work high-dollar accounts, aging claims, and denial codes with strong overturn potential first.

## Create Payer-Specific Playbooks

Each payer has its own filing windows, appeal rules, and documentation requirements. A concise playbook reduces training time and improves consistency.

## Report Appeal Outcomes

Track appeal success rate, average resolution time, and recovered dollars. These metrics show whether your denial workflow is actually improving collections.
