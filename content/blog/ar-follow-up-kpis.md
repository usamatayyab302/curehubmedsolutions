## AR Visibility Creates Better Action

Accounts receivable performance improves when teams know exactly where cash is stalled. KPI reporting should guide daily follow-up, not just month-end reviews.

## Days in AR

Days in AR is a baseline measure of collection speed. It becomes more useful when segmented by payer, specialty, and aging bucket.

## Aging Over 90 Days

Old balances deserve focused attention because collection probability drops sharply over time. Teams should review root causes for claims aging beyond 90 days.

### Pair Aging With Denial Data

Aging reports alone do not explain why cash is delayed. Combine them with denial trends to decide whether the issue is documentation, payer behavior, or follow-up discipline.

## Net Collection Rate

Net collection rate helps distinguish between timing issues and true revenue leakage. If this metric slips, leadership should inspect contract management, coding accuracy, and write-off controls.
