## Introduction

Medical billing directly affects reimbursement speed, clean claim rates, and cash flow stability. Small process gaps usually compound into avoidable denials and delayed collections.

## Verify Insurance Before the Visit

Eligibility checks should happen before the patient arrives. This prevents coverage surprises, catches authorization issues early, and reduces front-end rework for the billing team.

### Build a Pre-Visit Checklist

Your checklist should confirm active coverage, payer-specific referral rules, deductible status, and whether the scheduled service needs prior authorization.

## Standardize Charge Capture

Providers need a repeatable workflow for documenting services and closing charts on time. Delayed charge entry often causes billing bottlenecks and late submissions.

## Tighten Claim Scrubbing Rules

Claims should be checked for coding conflicts, modifier misuse, missing NPIs, and demographics mismatches before they ever reach the payer.

## Track Denial Trends Weekly

Denial management works best when teams review the root cause by payer, location, and provider. Weekly reviews make it easier to fix systemic errors instead of reworking claims individually.

## Align Billing and Front Desk Teams

Revenue performance improves when scheduling, registration, and billing teams work from the same documentation standards. Front-office errors usually show up later as billing exceptions.
