## Aging AR needs focused prioritization

Accounts receivable becomes harder to recover as claims move further from the date of service. Practices that do not segment and prioritize aging balances often spend too much effort on low-value accounts while high-recovery claims continue to age.

## Segment receivables by payer, age, and balance

AR recovery improves when teams break down receivables into clear work queues. Payer type, denial reason, claim age, and dollar amount all help determine which balances should be escalated first.

## Review underpayments as carefully as denials

Some claims are not formally denied, but they are still underpaid. Contracted reimbursement expectations should be compared with actual payment to identify cases that deserve appeal or reconsideration.

### Build repeatable follow-up cycles

Billing teams need a disciplined follow-up calendar for every major payer. Without a repeatable cadence, notes become inconsistent, timely filing windows are missed, and opportunities for recovery shrink.

## Use AR trends to strengthen future billing

AR recovery is most valuable when it improves future performance. The same analysis that identifies old unpaid claims can also highlight recurring front-end, coding, and documentation issues that should be corrected before claims age again.
