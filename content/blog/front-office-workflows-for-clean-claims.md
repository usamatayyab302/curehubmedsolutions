## Front-office accuracy protects the entire revenue cycle

Patient intake is one of the most important points in the billing process. If registration data is incomplete or inaccurate, clean claim rates usually decline and billing staff are forced into preventable rework after the encounter.

## Standardize registration questions

Every patient encounter should follow a repeatable intake script. Demographics, subscriber details, policy numbers, referring provider information, and coordination of benefits status should all be confirmed consistently.

## Verify coverage before the visit

Eligibility verification should happen before the appointment whenever possible. Confirming active coverage, deductible status, benefit limits, and prior authorization requirements reduces day-of-service surprises and claim submission errors.

### Train staff on financial conversations

Patients need clear explanations about copays, balances, and self-pay expectations. Front-desk teams perform better when they use consistent financial scripts and escalation rules for more complex situations.

## Create feedback loops with billing staff

The billing department sees the downstream effect of registration mistakes. Sharing denial patterns and intake errors with front-office staff helps teams correct issues early and improve clean claims over time.
